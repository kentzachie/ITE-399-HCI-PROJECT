STUDENT CAREER PATH

PHINMA Cagayan de Oro College was established back in 1948 as Parent-Teacher College – a nonpolitical and nonsectarian educational institution. The school served the youth and working individuals by providing education from the primary to the tertiary levels. In the next 30 years, PHINMA COC expanded its course offerings to include Computer Science, Engineering, and Mass Communications.

PHINMA COC was acquired in 2005 by PHINMA, making it one of the conglomerate’s first investments in the educational sector. Through PHINMA’s leadership and management, the college improved its existing academic programs by employing non-traditional approaches to learning.

Self-Assessment

Amidst the complexities of choosing a career path, self-assessment serves as a vital tool for students, enabling them to identify their passions, interests, and skills. Through self-reflection and evaluation, students can align their educational choices and career aspirations, fostering a clearer trajectory toward their desired professional endeavors.

What Kind of Student Are You?

Understanding what kind of student you are is an essential step in optimizing your academic performance and learning experience. By evaluating your study habits, learning preferences, and areas of academic strength or challenge, you can create a tailored approach to studying that enhances your educational journey.

Identifying Your Learning Style

Discover how you best absorb and retain information—whether visually, verbally, kinesthetically, or through writing. Knowing your style helps tailor your learning process, improving focus and success in school.

Improving Study Habits

Here are the top 10 ways to improve your study habits:

  1. Set clear, specific goals for every study session.
  2. Prioritize tasks based on urgency and difficulty.
  3. Engage in active learning—summarize or teach concepts to others.
  4. Use techniques like flashcards, mind mapping, or quizzes.
  5. Break study sessions into chunks with short breaks.
  6. Minimize distractions by silencing devices and staying focused.
  7. Ask for help when you're stuck—teachers or peers can guide you.
  8. Exercise regularly to boost brain function and focus.
  9. Avoid multitasking—do one thing at a time.
  10. Reward yourself after productive sessions to stay motivated.
